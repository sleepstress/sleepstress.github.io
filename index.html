STRESS & SLEEP
ABOUT OUR PROJECT

Advances in sensor technology and studies promoting mental health have shown a correlation between stress levels and sleep. In this study, we determined a correlation between variability in sleep duration and quality and stress levels during the day. We took three subjects and assessed their sleep quality by establishing an index related to 7 components including subjective sleep quality, sleep duration and so on.

Paired with operationalized stress data, we try to find a predictor of stress in order to determine a correlation with sleep quality to eventually develop a real-time intervention model. We had a 93% error but determined mean HR consistently delivered much more accurate F-scores than EDA feedback in detecting stress levels throughout the day.

"Among 100 college students, 89% felt stress was induced by irregular sleep patterns"

It is meaningful to keep students' metal health in mind as it is a habit forming portion of their life.

SENSOR
E4
LG smartwatch
E4 sensor:

Can collect heart rate, Blood Volume Pressure (BVP), Heart Rate Variability (HRV), EDA, temperature and Accelerometer data.


LG Smartwatch:

Used to collect stress level of our participants. The watch vibrates every hour, reminding our participant to answer the question "Are you stressed?" throughout the day.


OUR STUDY PROCEDURE

Here is our system structure:

Long-term goal: We hope to design a system that can detect stress in advance based on smart watch features so that the watch can intervene to reduce the stress and improve the sleep quality of the user.

Short-term goal: We found several participants to help us by responding to Ecological Momentary Assessments once an hour while wearing the E4 sensor. From this, we built an offline model to find the relationship between heart rate and stress level.

Data Collection: We had our three participants to answer our questionnaire about sleep quality assenment. Each person was required to answer for two days.

Our score is calculated based on the Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI). The figure above shows selected questions in the sleep survey. We can see that all the sleep scores for each two days are over 4, indicative of very poor all around sleep quality.

This graph indicates physical data collected by E4 sensor. Here shows the variation digram of temperature, Heart Rate and Acceleremoters. The sensor also can detect EDA and Blood Volume Pulse. In our project, we only focus on the relationship between the varation of Heart Rate and EDA.

DEMO & RESULT

Based on the data of our participants, we found that students who has good sleep quality are less likely to feel stress and subject to lower variations in stress and in heart rate. The more they felt stressed, the higher their heart rates were around the report.
